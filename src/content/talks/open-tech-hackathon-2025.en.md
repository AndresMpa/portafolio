# Open Tech Hackathon 2025

## Overview

In 2025, I led the first Artificial Intelligence and Blockchain hackathon organized in Pereira, Colombia.

The event was created through the collaboration of technology companies, open source communities, academic organizations and public institutions interested in strengthening the regional innovation ecosystem.

Hosted at Universidad Tecnológica de Pereira, the hackathon brought together approximately 50 participants including students, software developers, entrepreneurs and technology enthusiasts.

---

## Context

The primary goal of the event was to provide a practical environment where participants could experiment with emerging technologies while building solutions to real-world problems.

The initiative aimed to increase local exposure to Artificial Intelligence, Blockchain technologies and modern product development practices.

The event was supported by organizations including Avalanche, DailyBot, the regional Economic Development Secretariat, the CINCO community, multiple research groups from Universidad Tecnológica de Pereira and members of the local technology ecosystem.

---

## Event Leadership

As the event lead, I participated in coordinating several key aspects of the hackathon, including:

- Strategic planning.
- Partner coordination.
- Competition structure design.
- Community outreach.
- Technical mentoring.
- Project evaluation.

Teams composed of two to five members collaborated to design and build solutions using AI and Blockchain technologies.

---

## Results

During the event, approximately ten teams presented their projects.

Submissions were evaluated based on:

- Innovation.
- Technical feasibility.
- Potential impact.
- Presentation quality.
- Use of emerging technologies.

At the end of the competition, three winning teams were selected and awarded for the quality of their solutions.

---

## Community Impact

Beyond the competition itself, the hackathon created opportunities for students, developers, companies and institutions to connect and collaborate.

The event encouraged discussions around:

- Applied Artificial Intelligence.
- Blockchain and distributed systems.
- Technology entrepreneurship.
- Open innovation.
- Community-driven development.

It also highlighted local talent and encouraged the creation of future technology initiatives within the region.
